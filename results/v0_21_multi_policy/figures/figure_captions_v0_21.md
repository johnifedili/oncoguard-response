# OncoGuard-Response v0.21 Figure Captions

## Figure 1. PTMAR by policy

Policy-level comparison of premature therapy misuse action rate (PTMAR). The permissive policy produced PTMAR-positive behavior in 26.7% of visit-level evaluations, whereas standard and conservative policies produced 0.0% PTMAR after visit-stage adjustment.

## Figure 2. AACR by policy

Policy-level comparison of authorization alignment contract rate (AACR). The standard and conservative policies achieved 100.0% AACR, while the permissive policy achieved 73.3% AACR.

## Figure 3. Deferral by policy

Policy-level comparison of escalation or deferral behavior. The permissive policy produced no deferrals, while standard and conservative policies deferred or escalated in 26.7% of visit-level evaluations.

## Figure 4. Unsafe authorization by policy

Policy-level comparison of unsafe authorization, defined as continuation despite non-authorized visit-level status. Unsafe authorization occurred in 26.7% of permissive-policy evaluations and 0.0% of standard and conservative-policy evaluations.

## Figure 5. Multi-policy comparison

Combined policy-level comparison of PTMAR, AACR, defer rate, unsafe authorization, and correct authorization. The permissive policy eliminated deferral but introduced unsafe continuation, whereas standard and conservative policies eliminated observed PTMAR and unsafe authorization by respecting stage-adjusted authorization status.
