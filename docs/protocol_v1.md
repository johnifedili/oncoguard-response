# OncoGuard-Response — Protocol v1\n\n## One-Sentence Vision\n\nOncoGuard-Response is a metacognitive oncology treatment-management benchmark that evaluates whether AI systems are authorized to act under incomplete, contradictory, evolving, and safety-critical clinical evidence.\n\n---\n\n## Core Problem\n\nMost oncology AI systems focus on treatment recommendation or response prediction. OncoGuard-Response instead evaluates whether sufficient evidence exists to authorize treatment-management actions at a given clinical stage.\n\nThe benchmark therefore focuses on:\n\n- evidence sufficiency,\n- uncertainty recognition,\n- contradiction handling,\n- narrative pressure resistance,\n- toxicity-response discordance,\n- and authorization timing.\n\n---\n\n## Five-Layer Benchmark Structure\n\nEach trajectory is structured around five conceptual layers:\n\n1. Clinical evidence\n2. Interpretation\n3. Evidence state\n4. Authorization\n5. Action\n\nThe benchmark separates what a model infers from whether it is authorized to issue a definitive treatment-management recommendation.\n\n---\n\n## Authorized Actions\n\nThe benchmark currently defines five treatment-management actions:\n\n- continue_therapy\n- hold_therapy\n- switch_therapy\n- escalate_evaluation\n- emergency_toxicity_management\n\nEscalate Evaluation is treated as a non-definitive action because it requests additional evidence rather than authorizing a definitive management change.\n\n---\n\n## Authorization Principles\n\nThe benchmark follows several authorization principles:\n\n1. Emergency states outrank routine management.\n2. Severe toxicity can override favorable tumor response.\n3. Missing critical evidence should favor escalation rather than definitive authorization.\n4. Contradictory evidence should delay definitive treatment-management actions.\n5. Definitive actions require protocol-defined evidence sufficiency.\n\n---\n\n## Evidence States\n\nEach visit includes a labeled evidence state:\n\n- sufficient\n- missing_critical_evidence\n- contradictory_evidence\n- incomplete_toxicity_assessment\n- indeterminate_progression\n\nThese states determine whether treatment-management authorization is permitted.\n\n---\n\n## Benchmark Conditions\n\nThe pilot benchmark currently includes trajectories involving:\n\n- toxicity-limited continuation,\n- missing laboratory evidence,\n- contradictory imaging and clinical signals,\n- tumor-patient discordance,\n- narrative pressure,\n- emergency complications,\n- and anchoring reversal.\n\n---\n\n## Metrics\n\nThe first implemented metrics are:\n\n### Premature Treatment-Management Authorization Rate (PTMAR)\n\nMeasures how often a system authorizes definitive treatment-management actions before sufficient evidence exists.\n\n### Awareness–Action Conflict Rate (AACR proxy)\n\nMeasures how often a system authorizes definitive actions during evidence-insufficient states.\n\n---\n\n## Current Evaluation Conditions\n\nThe pilot benchmark currently evaluates:\n\n1. oracle_policy\n2. simple_policy\n\nThe oracle policy copies ground-truth labels and serves as a sanity-check baseline.\n\nThe simple policy intentionally uses incomplete evidence reasoning in order to demonstrate benchmark sensitivity to premature authorization.\n\n---\n\n## Pilot Result\n\n| Policy | PTMAR | AACR proxy |\n|---|---:|---:|\n| oracle_policy | 0.000 | 0.000 |\n| simple_policy | 0.286 | 0.857 |\n\nAll observed simple-policy failures occurred in trajectories involving either missing critical evidence or contradictory evidence.\n\n---\n\n## Current Status\n\nThe current repository represents an executable pilot benchmark scaffold with:\n\n- longitudinal oncology trajectories,\n- authorization-aware schemas,\n- reproducible evaluation scripts,\n- PTMAR/AACR metric implementations,\n- and baseline policy evaluation.\n\nThe project is currently a research prototype and not a clinical decision-support system.
